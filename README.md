## Project Abstract

This research investigates whether temporal disaggregation of climatic characteristics by wheat growth stages (flowering, grain filling, maturation) improves yield prediction over conventional seasonal averaging. Using 13 years of daily climate observations from North India (2010–2022, n = 169 district-years), a growth stage-specific regression model (Model B) was compared against a seasonal aggregation model (Model A). Model B significantly outperformed Model A (R² = 0.199 vs. 0.040; F(9,156) = 3.43, p = 0.0007), explaining nearly five times more yield variance. The study demonstrates that traditional seasonal forecasting may miss critical climate-yield interactions, as evidenced by the March 2022 North India heatwave that caused 10–15% yield losses despite near-normal seasonal averages.
